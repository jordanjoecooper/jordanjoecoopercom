Collection of quotes and aphorisms that I've found interesting or inspiring from various sources, typically in books but sometimes conversations, podcasts or blog posts

"I try to get rid of people who always confidently answer questions about which they don't have any real knowledge."
— Charlie Munger, Poor Charlies Almanack

"Take a simple idea, and take it seriously."
— Charlie Munger, Poor Charlies Almanack

"Don't let go too soon but don't hang on too long, find a balance."
— Morrie Schwartz, Tuesdays with Morry

"It takes 20 years to build a reputation and five minutes to ruin it. If you think about that, you'll do things differently."
— Warren Buffet

"Without numerical fluency, in the part of life most of us inhibit, you are like a one-legged man in an ass-kicking contest."
— Charlie Munger, Poor Charlies Almanack

"There is no bad weather, only inappropriate clothing."
— Sir Ranulph Fiennes, Mad, Bad & Dangerous to know

"If a god were to tell you, 'Tomorrow, or at best the day after, you will be dead,' you would not unless the most abject of men, be greatly solicitous whether it was to be the later day, rather than the morrow - for what is the difference between them? In the same way, do not reckon it of great moment whether it will come years and years hence, or tomorrow"
— Marcus Aurelius, Meditations(47)
