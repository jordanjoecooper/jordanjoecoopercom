Career Blindness

01/06/2026

My generation were told the right way to do things. That is, go to school, get as good grades as you can, go to six form or college and repeat, then go to university and do it again, once you finish that you can finally land a well paid entry level role in your chosen profession. Oh, you should also try to make that journey in a linear fashion and not get wrong too much from the age of about 15. 

Putting it like that I question the path for myself and frankly anyone who does not have a clear picture as such a young age. Now on the sheer number of people there will of course be some who do have a clear idea but I would wager a large number have no clue and get forced into this and that which leads to boredom and lack of fulfillment. 

How then do we resolve this? Well the education system itself was not designed to provide flexibility. Its a relic of the victorian era system designed to prep us for factory work , hence the timings which came from X YZ in history add later, now of course the UK deindustrialised hugely and now has a largely service driven economy (in London at least) although that is under threat from automation. How do we convince youth that education isnt a bad or boring thing (an age old question) and more over how do we give them as much exposure to interesting things as possible to help them figure out what they enjoy or have a natural aptitude for? (ideally both). 


Steve Jobs said that you should not become a career. The UK is setup to force you into that exact framing, reject it. None of us are a career , we are not meant to simply fit into a single box. Despite the drugging of us all via repeated engineered social media we are all completely unique our experiences individually are completely different to any other person on the planet in neat, subtle and complex ways, do not lose sight of that, you are not a career, you are you and there is only one of you, so find what the fuck you love to do and stick at it.
